  • First-Ever Blockchain Hackathon at UTDallas.

    HackDFW Proof-of-Hack is 24 hours for you to learn, create, and make. For the whole weekend, students will make innovative projects focused on blockchain and cryptographic technology with help from cryptocurrency sponsors.

  • Where is Proof-of-Hack?

    Proof-of-Hack will be held at the UTDallas Makerspace Building at the University of Texas at Dallas. With both the first and second floor, anticipate a large open space for community hacking and the quiet floor for sleep and concentration.

    The address is 3000 Waterview Pkway, Richardson, TX 75080

A hackathon is a weekend long event where projects are made from idea to prototype. At the end of the given time, projects are presented in a science fair setting—which, yes, includes prizes. The purpose of a hackathon is to have students learn development by creating a product while being provided resources like free-rentable hardware, tech workshops, and mentors. Hackathons are a space where road-blocks are eliminated, and students can learn new skills with ease.


Gather your friends, meet some peeps at opening, or attend our team-creation event right after opening ceremonies. No worries about knowing anyone beforehand; HackDFW is for creating new connections and new people from around the states. Maximum 4 people per team.
